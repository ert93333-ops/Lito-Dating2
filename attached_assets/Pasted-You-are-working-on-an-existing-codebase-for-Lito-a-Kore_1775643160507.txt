==================================================
AUDIT AREAS
==================================================

Audit the system across these layers:

1. Feature extraction correctness
- Are features being extracted from the correct message windows?
- Are partner/user roles handled correctly?
- Are timestamps interpreted correctly?
- Are silence gaps and re-initiation logic working correctly?
- Are translation-heavy conversations handled safely?

2. Stage detection correctness
- Are Opening / Discovery / Escalation states assigned consistently?
- Are thresholds configurable and applied correctly?
- Are progression signals falsely triggering escalation?

3. PRS computation correctness
- Are weights applied correctly by stage?
- Are penalties applied correctly?
- Is score clamping working correctly?
- Are there impossible or unstable outputs?

4. Confidence Score correctness
- Does confidence drop appropriately with low data?
- Does confidence handle long inactivity correctly?
- Does confidence handle conflicting signals correctly?
- Are low-confidence states properly suppressing overconfident UI?

5. Reason code quality
- Do generated reasons actually reflect the underlying signals?
- Are reason codes missing in important cases?
- Are reasons contradictory in mixed-signal cases?

6. UI integration quality
- Is the UI calm, useful, and non-creepy?
- Are strong claims avoided?
- Are low-confidence and mixed-signal fallback states working?
- Does repeated refresh cause flicker or inconsistent display?

7. Coaching suggestion quality
- Are suggestions based on actual signals?
- Are suggestions too generic?
- Are suggestions manipulative or overly deterministic?
- Do suggestions respect stage and confidence?

TEST SCENARIOS

Audit the system against at least these scenarios:

1. Very early conversation

2. High responsiveness but shallow conversation

Expected:

3. Warm and engaged conversation

4. High warmth, low progression

- no next-step signals
Expected:

- coaching should avoid pushing too early

5. Strong progression case
- availability sharing
- call/date openness
Expected:
- escalation stage or clear progression signal
- appropriate coaching suggestions

6. Mixed signal case
- slow replies
- but good topic continuity
- or warm tone with generic responses
Expected:
- mixed signal handling
- confidence-aware interpretation

7. Long silence + partner re-initiation
Expected:
- re-initiation should positively affect receptivity
- score should not collapse incorrectly

8. Oversharing or self-promotion case
Expected:
- penalties should activate appropriately
- not over-penalize harmless detail

9. Generic template reply case
Expected:
- genericity penalty should trigger
- explanation should remain user-safe

10. Translation-heavy KR-JP conversation
Expected:
- system should degrade gracefully when semantic confidence is lower
- not hallucinate strong certainty

11. High scam-risk pattern
- off-platform push
- suspicious repeated phrases
Expected:
- scam penalty support
- coaching should not generate normal romance escalation advice
